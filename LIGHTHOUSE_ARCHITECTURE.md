# Lighthouse Clinical Suite Architecture

## North Star
**Enter once. Pull forward everywhere.**

Lighthouse supports clinical judgment by collecting structured evidence once and carrying it through assessment, formulation, diagnosis, treatment planning, progress documentation, outcomes, and discharge.

## Core layers

1. **Clinical Evidence Map**
   - Presenting concerns
   - Symptom evidence
   - Course and severity
   - Functional impairment
   - Psychiatric and treatment history
   - Family psychiatric history
   - Medical and substance factors
   - Trauma and social context
   - Strengths and protective factors
   - Risk
   - Measures
   - Diagnosis and level of care
   - Client goals and treatment priorities

2. **Unified Clinical Story**
   - Chief Complaint
   - HPI
   - Clinical Picture
   - Functional Impairment
   - Psychiatric History
   - Family History
   - Medical / Biological Context
   - Trauma / Social Context
   - Strengths / Protective Factors
   - Measures
   - Integrated Formulation
   - Diagnostic Support
   - Treatment Direction

3. **Clinical Coach**
   - Page-specific questions
   - Clinician scripting
   - Dynamic symptom-based prompts
   - Clinical pearls
   - Documentation tips
   - Differential and rule-out reminders

4. **Compliance and Quality**
   - COMAR readiness
   - Clinical completeness
   - Patient specificity
   - Clinical integration
   - Golden Thread continuity

5. **Future pull-forward modules**
   - Navigator Treatment Planner
   - Beacon Progress Notes
   - Horizon Reviews and Outcomes
   - Discharge / Continuity of Care

## Separation rule
Chart-ready narrative, Clinical Coach guidance, and documentation-quality feedback are separate data products. Coaching language must never appear in chart-ready outputs.


## Lighthouse Experience Principle — Assessment Journey

Never tell clinicians they are missing information they have not yet had the opportunity to collect.

The live interface separates:
- Narrative: what the record currently says
- Clinical Coach: questions, scripts, and teaching
- Assessment Journey: where the clinician is and what comes next
- Quality Review: opportunities to strengthen the current work
- Final COMAR Review: whole-assessment readiness before signing
